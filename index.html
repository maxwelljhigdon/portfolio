Case Studies

Main Case Study: Ricoh Pro VC80000 Chiller Plumbing System

Role: Senior Hardware Engineer | Tools: SolidWorks | Timeline: 2023–2025

Problem Statement

The Pro VC80000 inkjet printer’s printhead cooling system required external chilling via an SMC chiller, split between two side printhead units. Improper flow or misalignment could lead to overheating, reduced print quality, or service failures.

Constraints & Requirements

  • Precise fluid path routing within tight printer frame.
  • Minimize downtime and simplify field installation.
  • Thermal stability to meet printhead tolerances.
  • Vendor components compatibility (SMC chiller).

    • Design Approach

  • Modeled plumbing in AutoCAD for precise spatial layout.
  • Used SolidWorks to simulate fittings, routing, and clearances.
  • Iterative coordination with firmware/electrical to avoid clashes.

    • Key Decisions

  • Routing configuration: Side-by-side split → minimized bends → maintained flow.
  • Materials: Selected corrosion-resistant tubing for longevity.

    • Analysis & Validation

  • Lab tested on pre-production level machines.
  • Tolerance stack analysis for alignment with printheads.
  • Field verification across multiple printers.

    • Manufacturing & Deployment

  • Detailed CAD drawings for service technicians.
  • Standardized installation guide distributed globally.
  • Vendor-reviewed for quality assurance.

    • Results & Impact

  • Currently deployed across 10+ Pro VC80000 systems worldwide, with more on the way.
  • Increased uptime and consistent print quality.

    Mini Case Study: Ricoh Pro VC40000 — Printhead Misting Mitigation

    Context:

    High-speed continuous-forms inkjet printer deployed globally.

    Problem:

    Ink misting accumulated on electronics above the printheads, leading to long-term reliability issues.

    Constraint:

    Retrofit-only solution with no printer downtime and compatibility across existing field installations.

    Decision:

    Designed a bolt-on misting mitigation bracket using existing mounting features and service access zones.

    Impact:

    Deployed across 120+ printers worldwide, significantly reducing electronics contamination in the field.

    Mini Case Study: Ricoh Pro Z75 — Lab Space Integration Study

    Context:

    Cut-sheet inkjet printer planned for installation in an existing lab environment with multiple large-format machines.

    Problem:

    It was unclear whether the Z75 could be installed without interfering with two existing printers and required service clearances.

    Constraint:

    Layout had to account for fixed room geometry, existing equipment footprints, and required access zones without relocating current machines.

    Decision:

    Modeled the Z75 and surrounding lab space in AutoCAD LT to evaluate fit, clearances, and service access prior to installation.

    Impact:

    Enabled confident installation planning, avoiding late-stage layout changes and minimizing disruption to ongoing lab operations.

    Mini Case Study: Wheelchair-Compatible Motorized Disc Launcher

    Context:

    Sponsored assistive-technology project focused on enabling accessible recreational disc launching for wheelchair users.

    Problem:

    Existing disc launchers required standing operation and lacked adjustability for users with limited mobility.

    Constraint:

    Design had to be operable entirely from a seated position, remain portable, and use safe, readily manufacturable components.

    Impact:

    Delivered a functional prototype that expanded accessibility for wheelchair users while meeting sponsor performance and safety requirements.
