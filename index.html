Case Studies

Main Case Study: Ricoh Pro VC80000 Chiller Plumbing System

Role: Senior Hardware Engineer | Tools: SolidWorks | Timeline: 2023–2025

Problem Statement

The Pro VC80000 inkjet printer’s printhead cooling system required external chilling via an SMC chiller, split between two side printhead units. Improper flow or misalignment could lead to overheating, reduced print quality, or service failures.

Constraints & Requirements

    Precise fluid path routing within tight printer frame
    Minimize downtime and simplify field installation
      Thermal stability to meet printhead tolerances
        Vendor components compatibility (SMC chiller)

        Design Approach

        Modeled plumbing in AutoCAD for precise spatial layout Used SolidWorks to simulate fittings, routing, and clearances Iterative coordination with firmware/electrical to avoid clashes

        Key Decisions

          Routing configuration: Side-by-side split → minimized bends → maintained flow Materials: Selected corrosion-resistant tubing for longevity Mounting brackets: Custom 3D-printed prototypes validated positioning

        Analysis & Validation

          Thermal modeling to ensure consistent printhead temperature Tolerance stack analysis for alignment with printheads Field verification across multiple printers

        Manufacturing & Deployment

        Detailed CAD drawings for service technicians Standardized installation guide distributed globally Vendor-reviewed for quality assurance

        Results & Impact

          Deployed across 10+ Pro VC80000 systems worldwide Reduced cooling system service calls by 35% Increased uptime and consistent print quality
